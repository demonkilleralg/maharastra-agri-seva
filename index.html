AGRICULTURE IN MAHARASHTRA

Maharastra State is divided in mainly 3 regions: Konkan, Vidharbha and Marathwada region.

  • Principal crops grown are rice, jowar, bajra, wheat, tur, mung, urad, gram and other pulses.
  • Groundnut, sunflower, soyabean are the major oil seed crops.
  • Cash crops grown here are cotton, sugarcane, turmeric and vegetables.

In recent years Maharashtra has become the largest producer of Onions (63%), Banana (75%), Mandarin oranges(75%), Tomatoes(42%), Pomegranates, Alphonso Mangoes (90%) and seedless grapes (78%). The state leads the sugar industry sector with about 150 productive cooperative sugar mills.

Geography

Geographical Conditions in each Region

Konkan Region

30,728 km²

Konkan extends throughout the western coasts of Maharashtra, Goa and Karnataka. It is bounded by the Western Ghats mountain range (also known as Sahyadri) in the east, the Arabian Sea in the west, the Daman Ganga River in the north and the River Aghanashini in the south.

  • Temperature in Summers rises up to 40 degrees Celsius and climate is humid in this period.
  • The climate remains cool and humidity is also low during the Winters.
  • Heavy rainfall is experienced in monsoon along with temperatures upto 30 degrees Celsius.

Major crops grown in this region are agricultural crops, including rice, millet, coconut, cashew nut, betel nut, amla, vanilla and pineapple.

Vidharbha Region

97,321 km²

Agriculture in Vidarbha Vidarbha region of eastern Maharashtra comprises of 11 districts of the Nagpur and Amravati Division. These include Yavatmal, Akola, Amravati, Wardha, Buldhana, Washim, Nagpur, Chandrapur, Bhandara, Gadchiroli and Gondia. The region occupies 31.6% of total area and holds 21.3% of total population of Maharashtra.

  • High temperatures in this region are not unusual and temperatures in Summer reach up to 47 degree Celsius.
  • Temperature hovers around 29°C and at night it feels like 16°C.
  • Rainfall is unpredictable with some years less than average and somtimes very heavy rainfall.

Mainstay of agriculture here is cotton, soybean, pigeon peas and chickpeas, most of these crops are rain fed.

Marathwada Region

64,591 km²

Marathwada is a region of the Indian state of Maharashtra.The region coincides with the Aurangabad Division of Maharashtra. It borders the states of Karnataka and Telangana, and it lies to the west of the Vidarbha and east of Khandesh regions of Maharashtra.

  • Temperature is high in summers up to 45°C and atmosphere is dry.
  • The coldest month is December, with an average high-temperature of 27.7°C (81.9°F) and an average low-temperature of 16.2°C (61.2°F).
  • Marathwada is affected by frequent anomalies in rainfall during Monsoon season, which accounts for almost 80 percent of the annual rainfall.

The main kharif crops in the region are soyabean and cotton. Sugarcane occupies 5.74% of Marathwada's total cultivable area which is also 28% of its total irrigated area.
